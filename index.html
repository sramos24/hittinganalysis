01 · EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

One Root Cause, Cascading Symptoms

The numbers and the video tell the same story from different angles: Brooklyn's torso is opening too early in the downswing, and her hands and wrists are paying the bill for it. Everything else — the flat finish, the roll-over grounders, the under-cut pop-ups, the velocity struggles — is a symptom downstream of that one problem.

Her Early Connection averages 117° across 144 swings — a perfect-position number is 90°. When she manages to start the downswing within 15° of 90°, her bat speed jumps from 51 mph to 60 mph. Same hitter. Same body. Just sequenced correctly. That 9-mph swing is sitting there waiting to be unlocked.

The mechanical thread

Imagine a swing where the front shoulder stayed closed an extra 0.05 seconds. The hands wouldn't need to "lead" — they'd be working with the body instead of trying to outrun it. The barrel wouldn't drag and have to be wrist-flicked into impact. The bat path would have time to plane out instead of cutting upward in a hurry. The attack angle would settle into the 10–12° range. Time to contact would tighten. Inside pitches would get on-time hand-paths instead of jamming jobs. Every problem on this page traces to those 0.05 seconds of front-side patience.

07 · THE 8-WEEK SWING PROGRAM

From Diagnosis to Action

The program is built around a single mechanical priority: delaying torso rotation until the hands have committed forward. Everything else — bat plane, time to contact, attack angle, low-pitch coverage — improves automatically when that one thing fixes. Each phase escalates the difficulty (tee → toss → BP → live).

How to Run It

Three sessions per week, 60–75 minutes each. Always retest 2–3 quality Blast/Rapsodo swings at the start (clean baseline) and end (fatigued state) of each session. Track the KPIs at the bottom of this page weekly. Heavy-load swings (overload bat) early in session when neurally fresh; underload bat (-25%) when fatiguing to maintain bat-speed signal.

PHASE 01
Body-Bat Sequencing — Restore the Connection
WEEKS 1–2 · TEE-DOMINANT
Goal: Drop average Early Connection from 117° toward 100°. Get her body to feel "still and closed" while the hands work first. We don't yet care about ball flight — we care about the feeling.
Wall Tee — Front Side Wall
3 sets × 10 swings · all locations
Set up with a wall or net 4 inches off her front shoulder. The constraint forbids early shoulder flight; if she opens, she taps the wall.
Cue: "Hands first, shoulder later. The wall is closer than you think."
Connection Ball — Tee Work
3 sets × 8 swings
Soft ball or rolled towel between front elbow and rib cage. Must stay connected through contact; if it drops before extension, the rep doesn't count.
Cue: "Squeeze the ball through the ball." Targets Connection Score directly.
Two-Tee Path Drill
2 sets × 10 swings
Two tees in a line, back tee with ball, front tee empty 6" lower. She must miss the front tee on her path. Forces hands-inside-the-ball and a downward bat entry into the zone — directly attacks the steep attack angle.
Cue: "Knob to the inside of the ball."
Pause Load (1-2-3 Drill)
3 sets × 6 swings
Coach calls "1" (load), "2" (pause and HOLD for full second), "3" (swing). The hold burns the closed-front-side feel into kinesthetic memory.
Cue: "Patient on the pause. The pitcher waits for you."

Phase 1 Conditioning Add-on (15 min, end of session)

Med-ball rotational throws (8 lb, 3×6 each side, focused on hip-shoulder separation). Cable or band anti-rotation work — Pallof press 3×10 each side. Goal is to build the physical capacity to keep the front side closed, not just the technique.

PHASE 02
Bat Plane & Attack Angle — Level the Climb
WEEKS 3–4 · TEE + FRONT TOSS
Goal: Drop Attack Angle from 18.7° toward 12°. Drop bottom-row launch angles from 30° toward 18°. Stop missing under low pitches. Phase 1 sequencing must be holding before adding Phase 2.
High-Tee Down Drill
3 sets × 8 swings
Tee set 4 inches above her natural mid-zone, with target net low and forward. She has to drive the high pitch on a low line. Trains a shorter, more direct path through the upper third.
Cue: "Top hand down through the back of the ball."
Low-Tee Stay-Through
3 sets × 8 swings
Tee at the bottom of her zone (knee-high). Coach holds a glove or pad at her belly button height — bat must not pass it on follow-through. Forces bat to stay through the ball longer with less upward exit.
Cue: "Through it, not under it." Directly addresses bottom-row 30° LA.
Reverse Foot Toss (Inside-Out)
3 sets × 8 swings
Coach feeds front toss from low-and-in angle. She has to stay inside the ball and drive it middle/oppo. Trains the inside-pitch path that has been her dead zone.
Cue: "Knob inside, barrel late, drive to right-center."
PVC On-Plane Drill
2 sets × 10 dry swings + 10 live
PVC pipe or stick laid along the pitch's plane (slight downward). Bat must enter and exit on the line, no more than 6" off plane. Builds On-Plane Efficiency directly (currently 46%; target 60%+).
Cue: "Stay on the runway."
PHASE 03
Time to Contact & Velocity Readiness
WEEKS 5–6 · OVERLOAD/UNDERLOAD + LIVE PITCHING
Goal: Drop Time to Contact from 0.20s toward 0.17s. Build readiness for 55+ mph pitching. Locked-in Phase 1+2 mechanics under increasing time pressure.
Overload/Underload Bat Series
3 rounds: 5 heavy / 5 normal / 5 light
+25% bat (heavier than her gamer), gamer, then -25% bat (lighter). Trains both strength and bat-speed signal. Always normal bat last so muscle memory is on the gamer.
Cue: "Same intent every set. Bat is the variable, not the swing."
Short-Distance Front Toss
3 sets × 10 swings · 12-15 ft toss
Standard front toss but coach moves up to 12–15 feet (vs typical 25). Effective velocity is ~50–55 mph at home. Forces shorter time to contact AND inside-the-ball decisions. Start at 18 ft for the first week, work down.
Cue: "Quiet head, hands first." Build it gradually — protect her face.
High-Velocity Machine Work
2 sets × 12 swings · 58–62 mph
Pitching machine set 3–5 mph above her current game velocity. Mix locations. The point is exposure, not pretty contact — she needs reps where she has to commit.
Cue: "Trust the load. Don't add."
Two-Strike Approach Drill
2 sets × 8 swings
Mandatory two-strike stance: choke up half-inch, slightly wider base, mental commitment to "spoil and stay alive." Builds the contact-first version of the swing she needs against velocity.
Cue: "Shorter is faster. Square it up."
PHASE 04
Pitch Recognition & Stickiness Under Fatigue
WEEKS 7–8 · LIVE PITCHING + GAME TRANSFER
Goal: Carry mechanics into competition. Close the cage-vs-game gap. Fix the within-session decline (currently loses 6.4 mph bat speed Q1 to Q4).
Live Arm — Recognition Reps
3 rounds × 8 takes/swings
Live BP from a coach or older pitcher with mixed inside/outside locations. She calls each pitch's location BEFORE swinging ("away, away, in, away"). Trains pitch tracking that the cage cannot.
Cue: "See it land. Don't guess location."
Outside Pitch Take-or-Hammer Drill
3 sets × 10 pitches
Live BP. Coach throws random in/out mix. She must take all inner-third pitches and hammer all outside pitches to right-center. Resets the brain to attack outside pitches (her strength) instead of fearing them.
Cue: "Plate is mine. Outside is mine."
Fatigue Stack Drill
5 rounds × 10 swings, no rest between rounds
Direct attack on the within-session degradation. Maintain mechanics through round 5 — coach watches Early Connection visually. If she opens early, the round restarts. Builds the habit of late-game mechanics.
Cue: "Every swing is the first swing of the game."
Velocity-Then-Slow Mix
3 sets · 5 fast / 5 slow / 5 fast
Machine at 60 mph, then 38 mph, then 60 mph. Trains the timing recovery game pitchers will demand of her. Most 14u hitters can do one speed, not the transitions.
Cue: "Same swing. Different load timing."
